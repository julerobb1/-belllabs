Bell Labs Museum Project

Status: Planning Phase - We're currently in the early stages of planning and fundraising for this important historical preservation project.

Preserving Telecommunications History

We are planning to establish a dedicated museum to preserve and showcase the groundbreaking innovations developed at Bell Labs and through the AT&T Long Lines network. This museum will serve as a living monument to one of the most important research institutions in American history.

Our Vision

Our vision is to create an interactive, educational space where visitors can experience the evolution of telecommunications technology firsthand. The museum will feature:

  • Original equipment and prototypes from Bell Labs
  • Interactive exhibits demonstrating key innovations
  • Archival documents and photographs
  • Oral histories from former Bell Labs researchers and engineers
  • Educational programs for students of all ages
  • Research facilities for scholars and historians

Property Needs

To realize this vision, we are seeking:

  • A suitable property of approximately 15,000-25,000 square feet
  • Ideally located near historical Bell Labs or AT&T Long Lines sites
  • Accessible to the public with adequate parking
  • Space for exhibition halls, archives, and educational facilities
  • Climate-controlled areas for sensitive historical equipment

Funding Requirements

This ambitious project requires substantial funding for:

  • Property acquisition: Estimated $1.5-3 million depending on location
  • Renovation costs: Approximately $2-4 million to create appropriate exhibition spaces
  • Exhibit development: $750,000-1.5 million
  • Artifact acquisition and restoration: $500,000-900,000
  • Operating endowment: $3-5 million to ensure long-term sustainability

How You Can Help

We're seeking support from individuals, corporations, foundations, and government entities who recognize the historical importance of Bell Labs and wish to help preserve this legacy. Ways to contribute include:

  • Financial donations: Contributions of any size are welcome and appreciated
  • Equipment donations: Original Bell Labs equipment or artifacts
  • Property donations: Suitable buildings or land
  • Corporate sponsorships: Named galleries and exhibits are available
  • Volunteer time: Help with planning, organization, and eventual operations
  • Professional expertise: Architecture, museum design, grant writing, etc.
