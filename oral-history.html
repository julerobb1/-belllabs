Bell Labs Oral History Project

The Bell Labs Oral History Project preserves the firsthand accounts of the scientists, engineers, and staff who made history at one of the world's most innovative research institutions. Through recorded interviews, we capture personal perspectives on groundbreaking discoveries, daily work life, and the unique culture that fostered innovation.

Bjarne Stroustrup

Creator of C++

Recorded: June 12, 2015

Bjarne Stroustrup discusses the development of C++ at Bell Labs in the early 1980s, its design philosophy, and how the programming language evolved to become one of the world's most widely used. He reflects on the collaborative environment at Bell Labs that made such innovation possible.

Jeong Kim

Former President of Bell Labs (2005-2013)

Recorded: September 3, 2018

Jeong Kim provides a unique perspective on leading Bell Labs during its transition into the 21st century. He discusses the challenges of maintaining Bell Labs' tradition of innovation while adapting to a changing telecommunications landscape and corporate environment.

Robert Lucky

Former Executive Director of Communications Sciences Research

Recorded: March 17, 2010

Robert Lucky shares stories from his four decades at Bell Labs, including his work on adaptive equalization for data communications and his experiences managing research departments. He provides insights into the Bell Labs culture during its golden age and the impact of the AT&T divestiture.

Data Communications Research Management 1960s 1970s
